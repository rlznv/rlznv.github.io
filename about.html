About Us

Who are we?

TagTutor was created by three Texas Tech University students in the spring of 2025 for the class, ENGL 3368: Designing for the Web.

Why did we make TagTutor?

We made TagTutor to help future ENGL 3368 students succeed in their class.

Signing up for an English class, students may not have expected to be making websites, especially by hand. Learning the foundations of front-end web development can be a huge learning curve, so we wanted to help students feel more confident in their front-end journey.

We also wanted to help students effectively study for the ENGL 3368 midterm exam. As much as memorization is important for the exam, reading and visualizing HTML and CSS is also a major component to doing well on the exam.

And, well, we also made TagTutor as our final project to showcase what we learned throughout the semester. TagTutor was created by hand using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, using the techniques taught throughout the course.

Where did we get our content?

Informational content for our website was primarily gathered from the course's textbook, Learning Web Design.

For our Exam Prep questions, we referenced our own exams and created new questions that would test the same concepts tested in the exam.
